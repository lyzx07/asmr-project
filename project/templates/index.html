Relax Your Mind, Body, and Spirit

"Coined in 2010, ASMR (autonomous sensory meridian response) is a relaxing, often sedative sensation that begins on the scalp and moves down the body. Also known as "brain massage", it's triggered by placid sights and sounds such as whispers, accents, and crackles."

My ASMR Experience

ASMR came into my life a while ago. When I first learned about it there weren't many, if any, YouTube creaters in this genre. Now there are hundreds with millions of views. Even before the craze started I could see the need for it. I remembered feeling that sensation occassionally before, usually when I was watching someone get their hair or make-up done or when watching something very relaxing. I wanted the feeling to last as long as possible even though I didn't actually understand what I was feeling. Now I watch ASMR videos pretty regularly to help me relax, or fall asleep, or even to help with anxiety. I definately have my favorites and my go-to's, but I still explore what else is out there. This page is just a reflextion of that. I have no degrees in this field, so this is purely derived from my experience and opinion. Even if you have never felt ASMR doesn't mean it's isn't for you because it can still be very relaxing and calming to watch for most people. Much like how it is relaxing to watch the waves roll in at the beach, or listening to a cat purr, hearing an orchestra or choir, or even watching the sunset.

Just a small little reference guide to your ASMR journey.
